Treecord

This vine is very common and found in almost any temperate forests as well as the mountains. it grows up to 100 ft in legnth. It takes two years for the burgundy stems to mature, by which point they have often fused together into twisted ropes with a diameter of 8 inches and are rough and gnarled.

The rough leaves are average in size and are a light green-brown with a gradient shift to an intense blue on the tips. The leaves are jagged and end in three tips.

The vine is flexible but very difficult to cut, so it is not regularly used for anything except in times of desperation when something rope-like is needed.
